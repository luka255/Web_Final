Georgian Culture

Georgia is known for its deep traditions, hospitality, and ancient heritage. Georgian culture blends Eastern and Western influences through food, music, language, and values.

Traditional Music & Dance

Georgian folk music is UNESCO-listed for its complex polyphony. Traditional dances like Kartuli and Khorumi represent romance, war, and celebration.

Georgian Cuisine

Khinkali, khachapuri, lobio, and mtsvadi are national dishes enjoyed at gatherings. Georgia is also one of the oldest wine-making regions in the world.

Language & Alphabet

The Georgian alphabet is one of the world's 14 unique scripts, known for its curvy, elegant shapes. The language itself is Kartvelian, unrelated to any major world language group.

Supra (Feast)

At a Supra, guests enjoy hours of food, wine, and heartfelt toasts led by a tamada (toastmaster). It reflects Georgia’s value of hospitality and community.
